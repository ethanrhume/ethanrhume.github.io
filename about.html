About Me

I am a second-year Master's student in Health Services at the University of Washington. The M.S. Health Services program focuses on building a strong skill set in research methodology and analytics, with the end goal of addressing population health issues through program implementation and policy change. A core philosophy is the importance of evidence-driven decision-making in public health and beyond. I center my own work around the human impacts of anthropogenic climate change, disasters, and resultant disparities. As our planet continues to heat up and environmental hazards become more difficult to predict, we all are responsible for stepping up to the plate and promoting rapid adaptation and resilience.

As a "storyteller" at heart, I believe I have an important role to play in this work. By communicating my own and others' research findings in compelling and accessible ways, I can drive engagement amongst the public as well as policymakers and be a catalyst for decisive action.

My background in healthcare helped me arrive where I am today. I worked as an EMT in New Orleans, Lousiana for several years. During that time, I treated patients through multiple hurricanes, a tornado, and the Covid pandemic. I saw the way these disasters combined with existing socioeconomic injustices to exacerbate health disparities. On moving back home to Seattle, I began to work in the Medicaid space as a community health worker. I helped patients navigate homelessness and chronic health conditions during extreme heat events and winter storms, often with no wider system in place to assist. It's these experiences and more which motivate my interest in environmental justice and policy work.
